# Every cargo e-bike insurance option in France, compared

**For a 3,040€ vélo cargo électrique longtail stored in a locked garage and secured with two locks outside, the French market offers roughly 15 viable insurance options ranging from ~190€ to ~407€ per year.** The best value-for-money options are **Laka** (zero franchise, zero depreciation, ~230–267€/year) and **Qover** (best roadside assistance via Europ Assistance, battery theft covered, ~265–275€/year). For families carrying children, **MAIF** is the only insurer offering truly comprehensive bodily injury protection for both cyclist and passengers — though at a premium price of ~350–390€/year. No single insurer covers everything; most families will benefit from combining a specialized bike policy with a Garantie Accidents de la Vie (GAV) for passenger protection.

Insurance for a standard cargo e-bike (≤25 km/h, ≤250W) is **not legally mandatory** in France — it is classified as a regular bicycle. However, with **320,000–500,000 bikes stolen annually** in France and e-bikes being 3× more likely to be targeted, insuring a 3,040€ cargo bike is strongly advisable. Your profile — garage storage at home plus two locks outside — qualifies for full coverage with every insurer listed below.

## The top-tier digital specialists deliver the best value

### Laka: zero franchise, zero depreciation, maximum flexibility

Laka (UK-based, Baloise-underwritten, launched in France 2024) stands out with a **unique mutualized pricing model** where you pay only up to a published maximum — actual monthly charges are often lower. For a 3,040€ cargo in Lyon, expect a maximum of **~230–267€/year** for the Classique formula (casse + vol + assistance), with a **50% discount on a second household bike**.

The headline advantages are extraordinary: **zero franchise on every claim**, **zero depreciation forever** (a stolen 5-year-old bike gets replaced at full original value), and **no commitment** — you can pause or cancel any day. Battery is covered even if stolen separately. Accessories including child seats are covered if declared. The 200€ assistance envelope covers taxi or replacement bike rental after an incident.

Key weaknesses: **no bodily injury protection** for cyclist or passengers, no RC, and no legal protection. Coverage outside the EU is limited to 48 hours (extendable via paid option). Some Trustpilot reviewers (4.1/5, 755 reviews) report post-cancellation billing issues and claim rejections related to non-compliant locks. Antivol requirement: **ART 2 stars, FUB 2 roues, or Sold Secure Silver minimum** — certification-based, not weight-based.

### Hepster: cheapest option but a critical cargo bike flaw

The German insurer Hepster offers the **lowest price on the market** at ~190–200€/year with 10% franchise (or ~250–270€ with zero franchise). Like Laka, it applies **zero depreciation forever** and covers worldwide. Unique among competitors, it covers component **wear and tear** (usure) after 6 months, including battery capacity loss below 50%.

However, Hepster has a **deal-breaking exclusion for most cargo bikes**: removable batteries stolen separately are not covered. Since virtually all cargo e-bikes have removable batteries, this creates a significant gap. Claims processing has also drawn criticism — some users report **2–6 month delays** and frustrating back-and-forth with customer service. Monthly contracts are fully flexible (1-month notice).

### Qover: best assistance for families, strong all-rounder

Qover (Belgian, NN Insurance-underwritten) charges ~265–275€/year for its omnium formula and pairs it with **Europ Assistance 24/7** — the most comprehensive roadside assistance in the market. Crucially for a family cargo bike, Europ Assistance explicitly covers **return transport for the cyclist, their children, and luggage** via taxi/VTC. A replacement bike allowance of 15€/day (3 days max) is included.

**Battery stolen separately is covered** — a key differentiator. Franchise is 10% with a **200€ cap**, and a "franchise anglaise" system means that for damage repairs exceeding the franchise, **the full amount is reimbursed with no deduction**. No depreciation for 5 years. A **20% discount** is available with a GPS tracker. The main drawback: **annual payment only** (no monthly option), and résiliation requires 2 months' notice to a Brussels address after the first year.

Trustpilot rating: **4.3/5 from 2,700+ reviews** — the strongest reputation score among specialized insurers.

## Sharelock and Bicytrust offer solid mid-range protection

### Sharelock: the only specialist with built-in bodily injury cover

Sharelock (French, Seyna-underwritten) is **the only specialized digital insurer offering protection corporelle** as a standard feature in its Confort formula (~305–325€/year). This covers fractures and hospitalizations up to **3,000€/year** for the insured cyclist. However, coverage for child passengers specifically is not explicitly confirmed — verify directly before subscribing.

Its innovative app-based system reduces the franchise from 10% to **5% if you photograph your locked bike** at each stop. Vétusté is less generous than Laka or Hepster: full value for 2 years only, then 1% per month (capped at 50% loss). Battery and partial theft (wheels, saddle) are covered. Trustpilot is strong at **4.4/5 (563 reviews)**, though multiple reviewers flag **aggressive renewal price hikes of 32–45%**.

### Bicytrust: excellent for new bikes purchased from partner shops

Bicytrust operates two tiers. The **PROTECT formula** (partner shops only, ~280–340€/year) offers **zero franchise and 3 years at full purchase value** — making it the strongest depreciation protection outside Laka/Hepster for the initial years. The **CONFORT formula** (online, ~216–249€/year) applies 5% franchise and 2-year full-value coverage. Both include **24/7 assistance with taxi return** and explicitly list **kit enfant vélo cargo** as a covered accessory.

Critical limitation: **battery stolen alone is not covered** in either formula. Bikes must be less than 1 year old (PROTECT) or 5 years old (CONFORT) at subscription. Trustpilot is the highest among specialists at **4.5/5 (149 reviews)**.

## MAIF is the premium choice for families with children

MAIF's standalone bike insurance (~350–390€/year) is **the most comprehensive product on the French market** and the clear leader for family cargo bike use. It is the **only insurer that includes bodily injury protection for both the cyclist and household members** (including children carried on the bike) as standard. Coverage includes:

- **Medical expenses, lost income (up to 3,100€), permanent disability (up to 46,000€), and death benefit**
- **Household support services** (cleaning, childcare) up to 700€ during recovery
- **24/7 assistance** within 1–50 km, with replacement bike (20€/day, 7 days max)
- **Accessories explicitly covered**: child seats, panniers, GPS, trailers, helmets
- **Battery stolen separately: covered**
- **Vol coverage 24/7** in all locations, no time restrictions
- RC available as option at 4.70€/month

The trade-offs: MAIF is **40–70% more expensive** than digital competitors. After the first year, depreciation switches to "valeur vénale" (market value determined by expert) with a **minimum floor of only 15%** of purchase price — significantly worse than Laka's zero depreciation. The franchise is moderate at **80–125€**. MAIF does **not require being an existing sociétaire** — the bike insurance is independently subscribable. Bike must be less than 7 years old.

Optional RC at 4.70€/month adds third-party liability specific to cycling, complementing MRH coverage. Protection juridique (legal defense) is included.

## Traditional insurers mostly fall short for cargo bikes

**Groupama** (~250–270€/year) offers reasonable pricing but has critical gaps: **no vandalisme coverage, no assistance, no bodily injury protection**, and outdoor theft is only covered **between 7h and 21h** — useless for a bike parked outside during an evening dinner. Battery stolen alone is not covered. Bikes must be purchased within 3 months to be eligible.

**Matmut** requires an existing MRH contract and only covers damage from **collisions with identified third parties** — falls, single-bike accidents, and vandalism are excluded. No assistance. Must subscribe within 60 days of purchase. The optional bodily injury cover (2.90€/month) is actually the most generous in the market (up to **750,000€ for permanent disability**), but the underlying bike coverage is too weak.

**Pacifica (Crédit Agricole)** and **AXA** are essentially **non-viable for cargo e-bike insurance**. Pacifica has no standalone bike product — only MRH extensions with insufficient plafonds. AXA's coverage through MRH **does not cover theft on public roads** (the primary risk), excludes nighttime theft, and provides no assistance. These should be considered only as complementary home coverage, not primary bike insurance.

**MRH-only options** (Macif ~40€/year, MAAF ~54€/year, GMF) can supplement standalone insurance for home theft but are wholly inadequate as primary coverage — plafonds are often capped at 3,000–5,000€, outdoor theft is excluded or heavily restricted, and there is no assistance or dommages coverage.

## Niche and newer entrants worth noting

**AssurancesVelo.com (PCG Assurances)** at ~240–275€/year is notable for using **weight-based lock requirements** instead of certifications: U-lock or folding lock ≥1.2 kg, chain lock ≥2 kg. This simplifies compliance — just weigh your lock. It includes **protection juridique** as standard (rare) and covers bikes in France and abroad, including during competitions. However, it's a relatively new company (ORIAS registered 2025) with limited track record.

**Cyclassur** (~230–280€/year) offers **zero franchise if the bike is marqué/gravé** — a strong incentive for FNUCI registration. However, in Lyon (classified high-risk), **two antivols are required for a VAE**, at least one from their reference list. This matches the user's profile perfectly (2 locks). The major weakness: no minimum depreciation floor means a 5-year-old bike could be valued near zero. Claims are processed in **72 hours**.

**APRIL Moto** (~200–260€/year) carries a Label d'Excellence but halves theft indemnification between 22h–7h — problematic for evening storage outside. Accessories are only covered if purchased simultaneously with the bike.

**Coverd** (~180–240€/year) comes from smartphone insurance and has the most **aggressive depreciation**: 2% per month from month 13, with a floor of just 30%. A 3-year-old 3,040€ bike would be valued at only ~912€. Must subscribe within 30 days of purchase.

**Decathlon/Evy** applies **zero depreciation** (strong) but charges **15% franchise on theft — the highest in the market at 456€** on a 3,040€ bike. The "Abonnement Mensuel" accepts all brands; the "Classique" formula is Decathlon-bikes only.

## Lock requirements and what they mean for your setup

Nearly all insurers use **certification-based antivol requirements**. The most commonly accepted standards are **FUB 2 roues** (French cycling federation), **SRA** (a new bike-specific SRA "Vélo" label launched July 2025), and **ART 2+ stars** (Dutch standard). Popular locks meeting these standards include the **Abus Granit X-Plus 540**, **Kryptonite New York**, and **Auvray U Force 10**. Expect to spend **40–90€** per qualifying lock.

The exception is **AssurancesVelo.com**, which uses weight-based requirements (U-lock ≥1.2 kg, chain ≥2 kg) — simpler to verify but may accept some locks that don't meet certification standards, and vice versa.

**Your 2-lock setup** is ideal: Cyclassur explicitly requires 2 locks in Lyon (high-risk city), and having two locks exceeds the requirements of all other insurers. Your locked private garage satisfies home storage conditions everywhere — most insurers still require the bike to be locked to itself or a fixed point even in a private garage, so attach one lock to the frame as a habit.

## What matters most for a family cargo bike carrying children

**Bodily injury protection is the most critical gap in the market.** Only MAIF covers passengers (children) as standard. Sharelock's Confort formula covers the cyclist's fractures but doesn't explicitly extend to passengers. Matmut's optional corporel is generous but sits atop inadequate base coverage. **The practical solution: subscribe to a standalone Garantie Accidents de la Vie (GAV)** — available from most major insurers for ~100–200€/year — covering the whole family for cycling accidents regardless of bike insurance provider.

**Battery theft coverage matters for cargo bikes** because batteries typically cost 500–1,000€ to replace. Laka, Qover, MAIF, and Ulygo cover separately stolen batteries. Hepster and Bicytrust explicitly do not — a significant cost risk.

**Assistance with child return** is addressed best by Qover (Europ Assistance explicitly covers taxi return for cyclist, children, and luggage) and Bicytrust (24/7 taxi return). If your cargo breaks down 15 km from home with two children aboard, you need someone to get everyone home — not just reimburse a receipt weeks later.

## Recommended strategies for this specific profile

**Best overall value:** Laka Classique (~230–267€/year max) + separate GAV (~100–150€/year). Zero franchise, zero depreciation, battery covered, flexible cancellation, reasonable assistance. Total: ~330–420€/year for comprehensive material + family bodily injury coverage.

**Best family-specific coverage:** MAIF standalone (~350–390€/year). The only all-in-one solution with cyclist and passenger bodily injury, siège enfant coverage, 24/7 assistance, and legal protection. Higher price, but eliminates the need for a separate GAV. Weakness: opaque depreciation after year 1.

**Best assistance for daily family use:** Qover Omnium (~265–275€/year) + separate GAV. Europ Assistance 24/7 with explicit child return coverage is unmatched. Battery covered. Strong depreciation terms (5 years). Downside: annual payment only.

**Budget option:** Hepster with 10% franchise (~190–200€/year) + separate GAV. Cheapest comprehensive cover, but the removable battery exclusion is a real risk for cargo bikes. Consider only if your battery locks securely into the frame.

## Conclusion

The French cargo e-bike insurance market in 2025–2026 is **mature but fragmented**, with digital specialists dominating on price and flexibility while traditional insurers lag behind on coverage scope. **Laka and Qover offer the strongest material protection** for a 3,040€ cargo bike; **MAIF alone delivers integrated family safety** including passenger bodily injury. The absence of a single product covering everything — material damage, theft, assistance, bodily injury for all passengers, and legal protection — means most informed buyers will stack a specialized bike policy with a family GAV. For a Lyon-based family using a cargo longtail daily with children, the combination of **comprehensive theft/damage coverage (Laka or Qover) plus a GAV for the whole family** delivers the best protection-to-cost ratio at roughly **350–450€/year total**.
