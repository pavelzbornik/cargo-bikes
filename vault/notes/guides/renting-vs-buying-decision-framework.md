# Renting vs Buying a Cargo Bike

A framework for deciding whether to rent or purchase an electric cargo bike.

## Financial Comparison

### Rental Cost

Formula: **Total rental cost = Monthly fee x Months + Buyback fees + Optional add-ons**

Rentals typically bundle maintenance, insurance, and support. Monthly fees for cargo bikes range from 80-150 EUR.

### Purchase Cost

Formula: **Total ownership cost = Purchase price + Maintenance + Insurance - Resale value**

- Purchase price: 2,000-6,000 EUR for most cargo bikes
- Annual maintenance: 200-250 EUR (2 services)
- Annual insurance: 200-300 EUR
- Resale value after 5 years: 30-40% of original price

### Break-Even Point

Ownership typically becomes cheaper after **3-4 years** of regular use, assuming reasonable maintenance costs and resale value.

## Non-Financial Factors

| Factor            | Renting                       | Buying                      |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **Upfront cost**  | None                          | 2,000-6,000 EUR             |
| **Maintenance**   | Included                      | Your responsibility         |
| **Flexibility**   | Limited to contract term      | Sell or upgrade anytime     |
| **Customization** | Limited                       | Full control                |
| **Risk**          | Provider absorbs repair costs | You absorb unexpected costs |
| **Commitment**    | Low, easy to stop             | Higher, capital locked up   |

## Decision Guide

### Rent when

- Testing whether cargo biking fits your lifestyle
- Cannot spare 3,000+ EUR upfront
- Prefer included maintenance and insurance
- Planning to use for less than 2 years

### Buy when

- Committed to 4+ years of regular use
- Want specific accessories and configuration
- Comfortable managing maintenance
- Break-even math favors purchase at your usage level
