## Taking a Cargo Bike on French Trains

A guide to transporting cargo bikes on SNCF regional trains (TER) in France.

## SNCF TER Bike Policy

SNCF permits bicycle transport on TER (Transport Express Regional) trains. Standard bikes are generally accepted, but cargo bikes face restrictions.

### Permitted

- Standard bicycles (touring, city, road)
- Folding bikes (preferred, especially if collapsible to 90x120x60 cm or smaller)
- Small folding scooters
- Reservations sometimes required (1 EUR fee during peak season)

### Prohibited

- Tandems
- Trailers
- Tricycles and triporteurs
- Longtail cargo bikes (extended rear frame)
- Non-standard configurations

## The Compact Cargo Bike Grey Zone

Compact cargo bikes and midtails occupy a regulatory grey area:

- They may technically meet dimension specifications for standard bikes
- Staff hold discretionary authority over acceptance
- Visual appearance can override dimensional compliance
- No published distinction between "compact utility bikes" and "cargo bikes"
- Enforcement varies by station, route, and individual staff

## Practical Tips

- **Measure your bike**: Know exact length, width, and height. Compare against wheelchair stowage dimensions (70 cm wide, 120 cm deep, 137 cm high) used as a reference
- **Travel off-peak**: Staff are more accommodating when trains are less crowded
- **Remove accessories**: Panniers, child seats, and cargo boxes make bikes look larger and more "cargo-like"
- **Be prepared for refusal**: Have a backup plan (drive, different route, different departure time)
- **Check regional variations**: Rules may differ between TER regions
