# Bafang

Bafang (formally Suzhou Bafang Electric Science & Technology Co., Ltd.) is one of the world's largest manufacturers of e-bike drive systems and components, founded in 2003 and headquartered in Suzhou, China. The company supplies motors, batteries, displays, and sensors both as OEM components to bike brands and as aftermarket conversion kits. Bafang systems are widely used across cargo bikes, commuters, MTBs, and fat bikes at a range of price points.

## Overview

Bafang has positioned itself as a high-volume, cost-competitive alternative to premium European drive system brands such as Bosch and Shimano EP8. While lacking the same level of dealer-network integration or software polish as those brands, Bafang motors frequently offer higher raw torque and wattage figures at significantly lower cost. This makes them a common choice for value-oriented cargo bikes and OEM integrations where power output and price are prioritised over ecosystem lock-in.

Production for European markets is partly handled at a dedicated factory in Poland, reducing lead times and import friction for EU-based bike manufacturers.

## Market Position

- **Vs. Bosch / Shimano EP8:** Bafang motors typically offer more raw power (especially M620, BBSHD) at a lower price point. Bosch and Shimano lead on dealer support infrastructure, firmware integration, and long-term parts availability. Bafang is more open to third-party tuning and customisation.
- **Vs. other Chinese brands (e.g. Tongsheng, CYC):** Bafang is the dominant brand by volume, with broader OEM adoption and wider global parts availability.
- **Cargo bike suitability:** The M620 Ultra and BBSHD are the most relevant motors for loaded cargo bikes, offering torque figures (160–170 Nm) that comfortably exceed most European competitors at equivalent price bands.
