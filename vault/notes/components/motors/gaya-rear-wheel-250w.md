## Overview

Rear hub motors at the 250W EU legal limit are a common architecture for urban and short-tail cargo bikes. Placing the motor in the rear wheel preserves a clean drivetrain layout and allows a standard derailleur or internally geared hub setup at the rear axle, though this can complicate wheel removal and limits chainline flexibility compared to mid-drive alternatives.

At 250W rated power, real-world peak output for hub motors in this class typically reaches 500–750W depending on controller and thermal management, providing meaningful assist for loaded cargo use on moderate gradients.
