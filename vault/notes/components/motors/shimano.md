# Shimano

Shimano is a Japanese multinational manufacturer founded in 1921, headquartered in Sakai, Osaka. Originally and still primarily known for bicycle drivetrain components (groupsets, derailleurs, brakes), Shimano entered the e-bike market with its **STEPS** (Shimano Total Electric Power System) mid-drive motor platform. Their deep integration between motor and drivetrain — particularly via **Di2** electronic shifting — is a distinguishing competitive advantage.

## Overview

Shimano STEPS motors are mid-drive units mounted at the bottom bracket, offering natural weight distribution and efficient power transfer through the bike's gearing. Shimano produces cargo-specific variants of several motor models, tuned for higher torque at low cadence to handle heavy loads from a standing start.

As of 2025–2026, Shimano holds an estimated ~13% share of the mid-drive e-bike motor market in premium test segments, trailing Bosch but competing on weight, drivetrain integration, and price-to-performance ratio.

## Di2 Integration

Shimano's STEPS motors are the only mid-drive system with deep native integration into electronic drivetrains (Di2). When paired with XT Di2 or CUES Di2 groupsets, the system enables:

- **FREE SHIFT** — gear changes execute when torque drops, protecting the drivetrain under load
- **AUTO SHIFT** — the system selects gears automatically based on speed and cadence
- Improved prediction algorithms and wider effective cadence assist range (updated in 2025 firmware)

This integration is particularly relevant for cargo bikes where riders frequently shift under load.
