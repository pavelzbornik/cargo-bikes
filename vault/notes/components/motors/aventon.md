# Aventon

## Overview

Aventon is a US-based electric bicycle brand that designs and sells a full range of e-bikes spanning commuter, cargo, fat tire, trail, and performance eMTB categories. The company has grown from a fixed-gear and road bike brand into one of the more prominent mid-market e-bike manufacturers in North America, known for combining accessible price points with app-connected features and proprietary motor development.

Aventon bikes are manufactured in China and sold primarily through their direct-to-consumer online channel as well as a growing network of retail dealer partners. As of 2026, Aventon is entering the European market with a German hub in Munich.

## Motor Product Range

Aventon produces and specifies motors across two main drivetrain architectures — rear hub motors and mid-drive motors — depending on the model tier and intended use case.

### Hub Motors

| Application | Nominal Power | Peak Power | Torque | Used On |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cargo / Utility | 750W | 1188W | 80 Nm | [[Aventon Abound LR]] |
| Commuter / Lightweight | 250W–500W | 500–750W | — | Soltera 3, Pace series |

The Abound LR's rear hub motor is paired with a torque sensor (internal), giving it smoother, more predictive power delivery compared to cadence-sensor hub setups. The bike ships as Class 2 (throttle + PAS to 20 mph) with an in-app Class 3 unlock (PAS to 25 mph).

### Proprietary Mid-Drive Motors

Aventon has developed its own mid-drive motor lineup under the **Ultro** family for higher-performance and trail-oriented models.

| Motor | Nominal Power | Peak Power | Torque | Used On |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Ultro X | 250W | 750W (850W Boost) | 120 Nm | Current ADV, Current EXP |
| Ultro S | 250W | 750W | 90 Nm | Trava (EU), trekking models |
| a100 | 250W (36V) | 750W | 100 Nm | Ramblas |

The Ultro X, introduced with the Current eMTB series in March 2026, is Aventon's highest-output mid-drive to date and includes a dedicated Boost mode that raises peak output to 850W.

## Market Position

Aventon targets the value-performance segment of the e-bike market — competing against brands like [[Rad Power Bikes]], [[Lectric]], and [[Trek]] at various price points. Their cargo offering (Abound, Abound LR) slots into the sub-$2,500 category for long-tail cargo bikes, making them accessible alternatives to European cargo brands.

Key differentiators include:
- App connectivity (Aventon app for class switching, ride data, motor tuning)
- Proprietary motor development (Ultro line) at mid-range price points
- Torque-sensor standard on cargo and premium models
- Expanding dealer network and EU market entry (2026)
